Code of Conduct

CODE OF CONDUCT FOR TERRAE VOLUNTEERS - WORLDPACKERS

1. Registration Terms and Conditions

Registration: Prospective volunteers must register through the Worldpackers application before joining the program.

Contribution: Volunteers are required to contribute $20 per day per person to support operational costs and local community contributions.

Payment System:

  • 50% deposit must be paid no later than on the first day during the briefing.
  • The remaining 50% must be settled when half of the stay period has passed.
  • Payments can be made via e-banking transfer or cash on-site.

Program Duration: The minimum stay is 1 week and the maximum is 12 weeks.

Minimum Age: Participants must be at least 18 years old or have written permission from a parent/guardian if younger.

Health: Volunteers must complete a health form and provide medical condition or allergy information before arrival.

Attitude and Adaptation: Volunteers are expected to be open to local culture and willing to adapt to the rural environment.

2. Provided Facilities

Accommodation: Volunteers will stay in a traditional local house throughout the program.

Meals: Provided three times a day, prepared by locals using traditional ingredients.

Transportation:

  • Shuttle service for operational needs within the Senduro area.
  • Free pickup from Klakah Station during office hours (07:00 - 17:00 WIB).

Nature Tourism: Tours to several natural destinations around Terrae as part of cultural and eco-tourism experiences.

Certificate: Upon completion, volunteers will receive an E-Certificate as recognition for their contributions.

Social Network: Opportunities to expand connections with local communities and international volunteers.

3. Program Schedule

Day One: Introduction and Adaptation

  • Program briefing and basic rules introduction.
  • Meet local residents and learn about the local culture.
  • Discuss the program and align it with existing issues.
  • Rest and recover after a long journey.

Day Two and Beyond: Flexible Schedule

  • Activities are adjusted according to ongoing projects and needs.
  • Volunteers have the freedom to choose their working hours, with a total of 4 hours per day in a flexible manner.

4. Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Respecting Culture: Respecting local culture, customs, and societal norms.
  • Active Participation: Actively participating in agreed-upon programs.
  • Cleanliness and Environment: Maintaining cleanliness and environmental sustainability.
  • Behavior Ethics: Refraining from actions that harm the local community.
  • Reporting: Reporting any issues to the program coordinator.

5. Prohibited Activities During the Program

  • Prohibited Substances: Bringing or consuming alcohol, narcotics, or illegal substances is strictly prohibited.
  • Discrimination and Violence: Any form of discrimination or violence is not tolerated.
  • Environmental Damage: Collecting or harming local flora and fauna is forbidden.
  • Leaving the Area: Volunteers are not allowed to leave the program area without permission.

6. Sanctions and Consequences

  • Minor Violations: Verbal warnings from the coordinator.
  • Major Violations: Expulsion from the program without a refund of contributions.
  • Legal Action: Serious offenses will be reported to the authorities.
