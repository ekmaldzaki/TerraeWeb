Everything you need to know about Terrae Volunteers

Prospective volunteers must register through the Worldpackers application before joining the program. This ensures official registration and access to program details.

Yes, volunteers must contribute $20 per person per day to support operational costs and community contributions.

  • 50% deposit due by the first day of briefing.
  • Remaining 50% payable halfway through the stay.
  • Payments via e-banking transfer or cash on-site.

  • Accommodation in a traditional local home.
  • Three daily meals.
  • Shuttle transport within Senduro, including free pick-up from Klakah Station (07:00 - 17:00 WIB).
  • Eco-tourism and cultural experiences.
  • E-Certificate upon completion.

Minimum: 1 week. Maximum: 12 weeks.

Participants must be at least 18 years old. Those under 18 require parental permission.

  • No alcohol, narcotics, or illegal substances.
  • No discrimination, harassment, or violence.
  • No damage to flora and fauna.
  • No leaving the program area without permission.

  • Minor violations: Verbal warning.
  • Serious violations: Possible expulsion without refund.
  • Legal matters may be reported to authorities.

  • Before arrival: Non-refundable deposit, except in emergencies.
  • After arrival: No refunds unless the program is canceled by Terrae.

Contact the program coordinator via the number provided upon arrival.

Everything you need to know about Terrae Guild House

Prospective freelance workers must register through the Guild House platform before joining. This ensures all applicants are officially recorded and can access job opportunities.

No, there is no registration fee to become a freelance worker at Terrae Guild House.

Freelancers receive payments based on a commission system. The commission amount is tentative and depends on the type of work performed.

  • Pick-up and drop-off service (availability depends on the area).
  • Opportunity to work in a dynamic environment with eco-tourism and community-based projects.

The duration depends on job availability and agreements between the worker and Terrae Guild House. Freelancers can take on multiple assignments based on their availability.

Yes, applicants must be at least 18 years old.

  • Engaging in unprofessional behavior, harassment, or discrimination.
  • Failing to complete assigned tasks responsibly.
  • Violating company policies or local regulations.

  • Minor violations: Warning.
  • Serious violations: Possible termination of freelance work opportunities.
  • Legal violations may be reported to the authorities.

If a freelancer cannot complete an assigned job, they must notify the coordinator as soon as possible. Repeated cancellations without valid reasons may result in removal from future job opportunities.

Freelance workers can contact the program coordinator via the provided contact number upon assignment.
